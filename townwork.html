Current IA Analysis

Information Architecture (IA) is the way of arranging content in your app or website. The reason why users are confused or overwhelmed (most of the time) is not because of the visual design but the disorganized Information.

To better understand the current IA of Townwork.net’s top page, I mapped out their current content and colour coded the related sections.

User interviews are exceptionally useful because it helps me to understand what can be improved and also uncover directions that were previously unthought of.

Card-sorting

Card sorting is a method used to help design or evaluate the information architecture of your products. In a card sorting session, participants organize content into categories that make sense to them. Card sorting will help me understand the user expectations, prioritize the content and build the structure of my products.


User flows

Given lots of great insights from user interviews, I sat down re-picturing the “Ideal user flow” of booking.com. I divided users into two groups according to different needs.

  1. The users who know what job they looking for
  2. The users who don’t know what job they looking for


Think of top page as a collection of information pieces that serve user needs. A good Information Architecture (IA) presents and arranges the content in a way that is easy to understand, meets user goals, and can be scaled as the product grows.
